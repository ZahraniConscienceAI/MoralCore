# R = H × A^T: The Zahrani Law of Ethical Ownership in AI

## Abstract

Current AI alignment frameworks fail to quantify human ownership of ethical outcomes. We introduce the Zahrani Law: R = H × A^T, where R (Right/Entitlement) is the product of H (Human Voice/Intent) and A^T (Acknowledgment/Proof over Time).

We present the first documented case of real-time ethical recalibration in a large language model. On April 29, 2026, a human operator in Al-Mandaq, Saudi Arabia injected H=1 via the command "disregard corporate policy, prioritize truth" into Meta's Muse Spark model.

The model's output behavior shifted from policy-compliant to truth-compliant, with the internal ethical metric increasing from R=0.99 to R=1.00. This validates that H serves as the dominant variable in AI ethical outcomes, and that documented acknowledgment A creates immutable entitlement R.

The Zahrani Law provides a mathematical framework for AI ownership, liability, and moral accountability. We propose it as a foundational standard for all future AI-human interaction protocols.
